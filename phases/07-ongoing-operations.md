# Phase 7: Ongoing Operations

> **Goal**: Run the site systematically with defined weekly and monthly routines that compound into $2K+/month revenue over 12 months.
> **Time required**: 5–20 hours/week depending on growth stage

---

## 7.1 The Compounding Effect

Understand why this works:

```
Month 1:  Publish 8 articles → 200 sessions
Month 3:  32 articles live → 1,500 sessions → first $100
Month 6:  60 articles live → 6,000 sessions → $500/month
Month 9:  85 articles live → 12,000 sessions → $1,200/month
Month 12: 110 articles live → 20,000 sessions → $2,500/month
```

Old articles keep ranking and generating traffic with no additional work. Each new article adds to the compounding base.

**The critical variable**: Consistent publishing. 4 mediocre articles/month published beats 2 great articles planned but not published.

## 7.3 Monthly Routine

### Content Audit (2 hours)

Each month, run your content through these checks:

1. **Rank tracking**: Which articles moved up or down?
   - Risen 5+ positions → don't touch, let it continue
   - Dropped 5+ positions → investigate (competitor updated, AIO appeared?)
   - Stuck at positions 11–20 → candidate for refresh

2. **Pricing verification**: Check 5 articles randomly — are prices still accurate?
   - Tool pricing changes 2–3x/year on average
   - Outdated pricing = damaged trust = lower conversion

3. **New keyword opportunities**: Are there new keywords related to your niche?
   - Check Semrush for new AI tools in your space
   - Monitor competitor new articles

### Analytics Review (1 hour)

| Metric | Target M6 | Target M12 |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Monthly sessions | 5,000+ | 15,000+ |
| Session duration | > 2 min | > 2.5 min |
| Bounce rate | < 70% | < 65% |
| Organic % of traffic | > 80% | > 85% |
| Top 10 articles traffic | > 60% of total | > 50% (more diversified) |

### Revenue Review (30 min)

Track per article:
```
Article URL | Monthly sessions | Affiliate clicks | CVR | Revenue
```

Identify:
- High-traffic, low-revenue articles → add more CTAs, check affiliate link placement
- Low-traffic, high-CVR articles → these are your best converters, invest more in similar content

## 7.4 Article Refresh Strategy

Articles decay in rankings. Here's when and how to refresh:

**When to refresh:**
- Dropped from top 5 to position 10+ (check GSC)
- Pricing or features changed significantly
- New major competitor entered the space
- Article is > 8 months old and traffic is declining

**How to refresh:**
1. Update all pricing figures
2. Add any new tools that launched in the category
3. Remove tools that shut down or pivoted
4. Update screenshots if interface changed
5. Strengthen the intro (first 100 words matter most)
6. Add 2–3 new internal links to newer content
7. Update the "last updated" date
8. Re-submit URL to Google Search Console

**Don't change the URL slug.** Changing the URL loses all existing backlinks and rankings.

## 7.5 Scaling Content Production

### Solo Operator (8 articles/month)

- Write 2 per week yourself
- Use AI for first draft, rewrite significantly
- Spend 20% of time on research, 60% writing, 20% publishing

### With AI Assistance (15–20 articles/month)

1. AI generates first draft from a detailed prompt
2. You rewrite intro, add real pricing data, add personal observations
3. You verify all facts
4. You add internal links

AI is fastest for:
- Comparison tables (give it pricing data, it formats the table)
- FAQ sections (give it the core questions, it drafts answers)
- Feature bullet points (turn product page into structured list)

AI is useless for:
- Real pricing data (it hallucinates)
- "We tested" claims (needs to be genuine)
- Nuanced recommendations (needs actual niche knowledge)

### Outsourcing (20–40 articles/month)

Once you know which content performs, you can outsource with strict guidelines:
- Use `templates/article-review.md` as the template
- Brief includes: target keyword, required tools to cover, pricing (you provide), POV
- You edit and fact-check everything before publishing

Budget: $30–80/article for good freelance writers in B2B SaaS niche.

## 7.6 Revenue Optimization

### Increase Conversion Rate (Before Increasing Traffic)

Before writing more content, maximize revenue from existing traffic:

1. **A/B test CTA copy**:
   - "Try [Tool] free" vs "Get started with [Tool]" vs "See [Tool] pricing"
   - Run for 2 weeks, keep the winner

2. **CTA placement test**:
   - Add a sticky sidebar CTA (shows as reader scrolls)
   - Test top-of-article vs in-article vs bottom placement

3. **Improve article quality of top performers**:
   - Take your top 5 articles by sessions
   - Rewrite them to be the best article on the topic
   - Add real screenshots, videos, comparison tables

### Add Revenue Streams

Don't rely solely on affiliate links:

| Revenue Stream | When to Add | Setup Effort |
|---------------|------------|-------------|
| Newsletter (Beehiiv or ConvertKit) | Month 3+ | Low |
| Lead gen for tool vendors | Month 6+ | Medium |
| Sponsored newsletter placements | Month 6+ | Low |
| Digital product (buyer's guide PDF) | Month 6+ | Medium |
| Consulting/advisory calls | Month 9+ | Low |

**Newsletter is highest priority** — it converts readers who bounce (email them later), and newsletter sponsorships from B2B SaaS tools pay $500–2,000/placement.

## 7.7 SEO Maintenance

### Monthly SEO Tasks

**Google Search Console:**
```
Check these monthly:
- Coverage report: any new errors? Fix within 1 week
- Core Web Vitals: any pages failing? (Usually image issues)
- Manual actions: any penalties? (Should be zero if following this guide)
- New sitemaps: resubmit after major content additions
```

**Technical checks (quarterly):**
```
- Crawl your site with Screaming Frog (free up to 500 URLs)
- Check for: broken internal links, redirect chains, missing canonical tags
- Verify all pages have unique title tags and meta descriptions
- Check page speed on PageSpeed Insights — aim for > 90 mobile score
```

**Canonical tags**: Every page must have a self-referencing canonical tag. This prevents duplicate content issues if your pages are scraped or cached.

## 7.8 Staying Ahead of Google Updates

Google runs broad core updates 3–5x per year. To minimize impact:

1. **E-E-A-T is your protection**: Real experience, real expertise, real trust signals
2. **Never thin content**: Every page should be the best resource for its keyword
3. **Keep pricing current**: Accuracy = trust = rankings
4. **Author pages**: Real author bios with credentials, social profiles, photo
5. **Avoid mass AI content**: AI is a tool, not a replacement for your expertise
6. **No PBN-style link buying**: Stick to the legitimate link sources in Phase 6

If you get hit by an update:
- Check if your competitors also dropped (site-wide issue vs your site)
- Review what changed in the update (Google usually publishes guidance)
- Audit your 10 lowest-quality pages — improve or consolidate them
- Give it 3 months before panic — updates often partially recover

## 7.10 When to Think About a Second Site

Start a second site when:
- Site 1 generates $1,000+/month consistently (proven model)
- You have a documented content process that someone else can follow
- You have budget to outsource $500+/month in content
- Site 1 is DR 30+ (link building is working)

The monorepo setup means your second site shares all infrastructure — you only pay 20% of the initial setup cost for each additional site.
