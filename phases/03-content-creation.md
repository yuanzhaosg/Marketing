# Phase 3: Content Creation

> **Goal**: Publish 20 high-quality articles targeting low-KD commercial keywords, establishing topical authority before going after higher-competition terms.
> **Duration**: Month 1–3 (ongoing)
> **Output**: 20 published articles, internal link structure, topical authority foundation

---

## 3.1 Content Strategy Foundation

### The Core Principle

Every article must answer one question: **"Which software should I buy?"**

You are not a blog. You are a buying guide. Every piece of content should move a reader closer to a purchasing decision, even if they're in the awareness stage.

### Content Hierarchy

```
Pillar Pages (1 per niche)
└── "Best [Category] Software in 2026" — 3,000–5,000 words
    ├── Alternative Pages (10–20 per pillar)
    │   └── "[Tool Name] Alternatives" — 2,000–3,000 words
    ├── Comparison Pages (5–15 per pillar)
    │   └── "[Tool A] vs [Tool B]" — 1,500–2,500 words
    ├── Review Pages (10–30 per pillar)
    │   └── "[Tool Name] Review" — 2,000–3,000 words
    └── Vertical Pages (as needed)
        └── "Best [Category] Software for [Industry]" — 2,000 words
```

## 3.2 Content Prioritization — Write These First

Order by: **KD ≤ 20 first, then KD 20–35.**

### Week 1–2: Alternatives Pages (Fastest to Rank)

Alternatives pages rank fastest because:
- Lower KD than "best X software" (usually KD 10–25 vs KD 30–50)
- High commercial intent (user already knows the category)
- Easy to structure (numbered list format)

```
Target pattern: "[Tool Name] alternatives"
Example: "gusto alternatives", "zendesk alternatives", "quickbooks alternatives"

Structure:
1. Why people look for alternatives (pricing, features, limitations)
2. Quick comparison table (tool, price, best for)
3. #1 alternative — detailed section
4. #2 alternative — detailed section
... (7–10 alternatives)
N. FAQ
N+1. Bottom line (your recommendation with affiliate CTA)
```

### Week 3–4: VS Pages (Second Fastest)

```
Target pattern: "[Tool A] vs [Tool B]"
Example: "gusto vs rippling", "zendesk vs freshdesk"

Structure:
1. Quick verdict (who should use which)
2. Side-by-side comparison table
3. [Tool A] strengths
4. [Tool B] strengths
5. Pricing comparison (include the sneaky add-ons!)
6. Integrations and ecosystem
7. Who should choose [Tool A]
8. Who should choose [Tool B]
9. Bottom line with CTAs to both
```

### Month 2: Best X Pillar Pages

```
Target pattern: "best [category] software"
Example: "best payroll software", "best help desk software"

Structure:
1. Quick picks table (tool, starting price, best for, our score)
2. How we tested and selected
3. #1 pick — detailed review (features, pricing, pros/cons, who it's for)
4. #2 pick — detailed review
... (7–12 tools)
N. What to look for when choosing
N+1. Pricing comparison table
N+2. FAQ
N+3. Bottom line
```

### Month 3: Review Pages (Long-tail, Brand Searches)

```
Target pattern: "[Tool Name] review"
Example: "gusto review", "zendesk review"

Structure:
1. Overall score (X/10) with summary
2. What is [Tool]? (who it's for)
3. Key features walkthrough
4. Pricing (with actual numbers, including hidden costs)
5. What we like
6. What we don't like
7. Who it's best for / who should avoid it
8. Alternatives to consider
9. Bottom line with CTA
```

## 3.3 Article Writing Guidelines

### Word Count by Type

| Article Type | Target Word Count |
|-------------|------------------|
| Best X (pillar) | 3,000–5,000 |
| Alternatives | 2,000–3,000 |
| vs (comparison) | 1,500–2,500 |
| Review | 2,000–3,000 |
| How-to guide | 1,500–2,500 |

### E-E-A-T Signals (Critical for Ranking)

Every article must include signals that Google can evaluate:

**Experience**:
- "In our testing..." (say how you tested, even if brief)
- Specific observations: "The setup took us 23 minutes" vs "setup is fast"
- Screenshots of actual interfaces
- Specific version numbers, dates tested

**Expertise**:
- Accurate pricing (verify on each tool's pricing page)
- Feature accuracy (don't invent features)
- Note what the tool *doesn't* do well — builds trust

**Authoritativeness**:
- Author bio with relevant background
- Links to credible sources for data claims
- Cite actual customer numbers, G2 ratings

**Trustworthiness**:
- Affiliate disclosure at the top of every article
- Last-updated date (keep articles current)
- No "sponsored rankings" language
- Contact page working

### Anti-AI-Detection Writing Rules

AI-written content is detectable. Avoid these patterns:

**Never use these phrases:**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "This is particularly important because..."
- "Delve into", "embark on", "navigate"
- "Comprehensive solution", "game-changer", "robust"

**Structural tells to avoid:**
- Every H2 starting with an adverb ("Importantly,", "Notably,")
- The exact same sentence length pattern throughout
- Conclusion paragraphs that summarize everything already said
- Em dashes used decoratively throughout

**What good affiliate copy looks like:**
```
Bad (AI):  "Gusto offers a comprehensive suite of payroll features..."
Good:      "Gusto handles payroll in all 50 states. The basic plan is $40/month 
            plus $6/employee — for a team of 10, that's $100/month."

Bad (AI):  "When considering alternatives, it's worth noting that pricing varies 
            significantly across providers."
Good:      "Gusto's $6/employee fee adds up fast. At 20 employees, you're paying 
            $160/month just in base payroll. Rippling starts at $8/user/month all-in, 
            which beats Gusto once you're over 15 people."
```

### Pricing Research (Do This Every Time)

**Never guess or use cached prices.** For every tool:

1. Visit the pricing page directly
2. Record: starting price, price at 10 users, price at 50 users
3. Note any fees not in the headline price (setup fees, per-payroll fees, add-ons)
4. Check if pricing has changed recently (sometimes companies update without announcement)
5. Screenshot the pricing page with date in filename: `tool-pricing-2026-05.png`

## 3.4 Internal Linking Structure

Build topical clusters through internal links:

```
Best Payroll Software (pillar)
    ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑ ↑
Gusto Review  |  Rippling Review  |  Gusto Alternatives  |  Gusto vs Rippling
    ↑                                      ↑
Gusto for Small Business         Best Payroll Software for Startups
```

Rules:
- Every article links back to its parent pillar page
- Every alternatives article links to the corresponding review pages
- Every vs article links to both individual review pages
- Use descriptive anchor text (not "click here")
- 3–5 internal links per article is healthy

## 3.5 Affiliate CTA Placement

Where to place CTAs for maximum click-through:

1. **After the quick picks table** (near the top, for readers who've already decided)
2. **After each tool review section** (in-context when the reader is most interested)
3. **Before the FAQ** (catches readers who scrolled down)
4. **Bottom of article** (catches readers who read the whole thing)

CTA copy that works:
```
✅ "Try Gusto free for 30 days →"
✅ "Get started with Rippling ($0 setup fee) →"
✅ "See Freshdesk pricing →"

❌ "Click here to learn more"
❌ "Visit website"
❌ "Check it out"
```

## 3.7 Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing each article:

**Research**
- [ ] All pricing verified on tool's current pricing page
- [ ] Features verified (not guessed from older articles)
- [ ] Tested or at least used the free trial/demo

**SEO**
- [ ] Primary keyword in title (near the beginning)
- [ ] Primary keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Primary keyword in one H2
- [ ] Meta description written (150–160 chars, includes keyword)
- [ ] URL slug is clean (`/gusto-alternatives/`)
- [ ] 3–5 internal links to related content

**Trust**
- [ ] Affiliate disclosure at top of article
- [ ] Last-updated date set
- [ ] Author byline present
- [ ] No fake scores or fabricated data

**Conversion**
- [ ] 3–4 CTAs placed at key scroll points
- [ ] CTA links use affiliate tracking (UTM or affiliate ID)
- [ ] Mobile CTA test (does it look right on phone?)
