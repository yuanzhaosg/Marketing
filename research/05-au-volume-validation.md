### Key SERP findings
- **EOR**: most winnable B2B affiliate niche observed — but the best money terms (`deel vs remote`, `best eor services`) are **global SERPs**, not AU-protected, and **crowded with motivated affiliate incumbents**. AU-specific terms (`employer of record australia`) are smaller but cleaner.
- **Accounting**: the AU comparison giants (Finder, Forbes Advisor AU, Money.com.au, savvy, Canstar) have **not colonised B2B software reviews** — gatekeepers are only vendor pages (xero.com/myob.com) + Gartner aggregators (Capterra/GetApp) on `{brand} alternatives`. Lead with `best invoicing software australia`, `best bookkeeping software for small business australia`, `xero vs myob`.
- **AI Overviews appear on ~95% of commercial queries** in all three clusters → organic CTR compressed. You must be *cited inside* the AIO (structured comparison tables), not merely rank.
- **Skip**: `how to hire an employee in australia` (locked by business.gov.au + Xero), `payroll outsourcing australia` (domestic-bureau intent, wrong fit), unqualified `docusign alternatives` (DR70+ global lock).

## Revenue reality check (honest)

The playbook's hard floor is **cluster SV ≥ 300,000**. The combined AU clusters are **~12K–31K — roughly 10–25× below that floor.** By raw volume, a standalone AU site **fails the playbook's volume gate.**

It only works because of **payout asymmetry**: the playbook's 300K assumes ~$80 commissions. AU EOR pays **US$1,500** (≈19×). Rough mature-state (M12) math:
- EOR ~5,000 SV → 8% share ≈ 400 sessions → ~10% CTR ≈ 40 clicks → ~1.5% conv ≈ 0.6 conversions × US$1,500 ≈ **~US$900/mo** (lumpy)
- Accounting ~15,000 SV → 5% share ≈ 750 sessions → Xero/MYOB conversions ≈ **~A$300–500/mo** (Xero recurring builds over time)
- E-sign ≈ **~A$100–200/mo**

→ Plausible mature range **~A$1,500–3,000/mo**, but **heavily dependent on lumpy EOR conversions** and a 9–12 month ramp. Achievable, not comfortable.

## ⚠️ Strategic flag: the money anchor overlaps your existing sites

The only thing that makes a *standalone* AU site pencil out is **EOR / cross-border hiring** — which is **already the core of two sites you're building**:
- **chinaabroadguide.com** (企业出海) = Chinese companies hiring/expanding abroad = *literally* EOR + global payroll. "Hire in Australia" is a sub-chapter of this site.
- **hrpaypick.com** = HR & payroll, the same Deel/Remote programs.

So a standalone AU EOR site would compete with **your own** chinaabroadguide for the exact terms (Google dislikes one owner targeting the same terms with two sites; it also splits your effort). What's *left* uniquely for a standalone AU site — AU accounting (Xero) + AU e-sign — is the **thin, lower-payout** half.

**Implication:** the validation technically supports "standalone AU site is winnable," but the economics quietly point back to either (a) an **Australia chapter inside chinaabroadguide** (captures the $1,500 EOR money with a site you already have), or (b) an **AU section in hrpaypick**. A standalone site makes sense only if you want a *separate, English-AU-audience* property distinct from the Chinese-language chinaabroadguide — accepting that its EOR money pages will overlap.

## If you proceed standalone — recommended structure

1. **Anchor:** EOR / hire-in-Australia (money). Pages: `best employer of record australia`, `deel vs remote`, `best eor services`, `eor australia`.
2. **Traffic layer:** AU accounting. Pages: `xero vs myob`, `best accounting software australia`, `best invoicing software australia`, `xero alternatives australia`, use-case pages (sole trader / tradie / freelancer).
3. **Support:** e-sign long-tail (`docusign alternatives australia`, `annature alternatives`).
4. **Moat (how you rank):** real AU-regulatory depth — superannuation, Fair Work/NES, STP, payroll tax, ETA 1999, BAS/GST, EOFY. International DR-70 pages handle these generically; you beat them on specificity.
5. **AIO defence:** structured comparison tables + direct-answer blocks so you're cited inside AI Overviews.
