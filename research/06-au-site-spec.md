# Standalone AU Site — Build Spec

> Decision: build a standalone, **English-language, Australia-audience** affiliate site. Anchored on EOR/hire-in-Australia (money) + AU accounting (traffic) + e-sign (support). Validated in `05-au-volume-validation.md`.

---

## 1. Positioning (and how it avoids cannibalising chinaabroadguide)

| | This AU site | chinaabroadguide.com |
|---|---|---|
| Language | English | Chinese (中文) |
| Audience | Australian SMBs + foreign cos hiring **into** AU | Chinese cos expanding **out** to the world |
| SERPs | en-AU Google | Chinese-language / Baidu / cross-border |
| Shared | Deel/Remote affiliate programs | Deel/Remote affiliate programs |

Different language + different search intent = **no SERP overlap**, even though both monetise EOR. Keep product comparisons distinct (this site: AU-localised; chinaabroadguide: China-outbound framing). Don't cross-link the two publicly (avoid linking your own properties in a footprint).

**One-line positioning:** *"The independent guide to running and staffing a business in Australia — payroll, accounting, hiring and compliance software, reviewed for Australian rules."*

---

## 2. Domain naming (your `-pick` convention)

Suggested, in priority order — check availability:
- **ozhirepick.com** (leans EOR/hiring — the money anchor)
- **aussiebizpick.com** (broad: covers hiring + accounting + e-sign)
- **payrollpickau.com** / **ozpayrollpick.com**
- **hireaustraliapick.com**

Notes: a **`.com.au`** boosts AU SERP trust but requires an ABN (you have one via your businesses) and a name match. A clean `.com` is fine and more flexible. Recommend grabbing the `.com`; optionally the `.com.au` too for trust signal + redirect.

## 6. The calculator (Site C tool, folded into this site)

**True Cost of an Australian Employee** — `/tools/true-cost-of-an-australian-employee`. Computes employee vs contractor vs EOR fully-loaded cost incl. **superannuation, state payroll tax, WorkCover, leave loading**. CTAs → Deel/Remote (hire) + Xero/MYOB (payroll). Per-state pages (`/nsw`, `/vic`…) for programmatic surface. Full spec in `03-site-c-tool-spec.md`. This is your primary **link magnet** — AU accountants/HR blogs cite super + payroll-tax numbers constantly.

## 7. Tech stack (matches the playbook + your existing sites)

- **Astro 5.x + Tailwind**, hosted **Cloudflare Pages** (free). Fits the monorepo in `phases/02-tech-stack-setup.md` — add as `apps/<au-site>` alongside your existing 4, reusing `packages/ui` + `packages/content-utils`.
- **Calculator**: Astro island + client-side JS; per-state data from a JSON file. No backend → stays free.
- **Schema**: `ItemList`/`Review`/`FAQPage` JSON-LD on comparison pages; `SoftwareApplication` on the tool.
- **Analytics**: Microsoft Clarity (free).
- **AU signals**: `.com.au` (optional), AUD pricing in tables, AU spelling, AU business examples, cite ATO/Fair Work/ag.gov.au sources.

## 8. AU-regulatory moat (how you actually rank vs DR-70 global pages)

Weave these into articles — international competitors handle them generically; you go deep:
- **Superannuation** (11.5%→12% guarantee, contractor super rules)
- **Single Touch Payroll (STP) Phase 2** reporting
- **State payroll tax** thresholds (NSW/VIC/QLD/WA/SA differ)
- **Fair Work / NES / Modern Awards** (hiring obligations)
- **BAS / GST**, **EOFY** workflows
- **ETA 1999** (e-signature legality)

---

## 9. AIO defence (AI Overviews hit ~95% of these commercial queries)

- Lead each money page with a **direct-answer summary + structured comparison table** (Google lifts these into AIO with citation).
- Use clear `<table>` markup, explicit "Best for X" verdicts, and FAQ schema.
- Target being *cited inside* the AIO, not just ranking #5.

## 10. Launch sequence (Phase 5 — free DR links)

1. Publish P1 articles (1–9) + the calculator.

5. HARO / AU journalist queries on hiring-cost + super.

→ Target: **DR 20+ in 6 months**, calculator carrying natural links, EOR money pages carrying revenue.
